The Game That Gave Dallas A Championship...

Luka Dončić and the Mavericks aren’t just the NBA’s future. They’ve arrived.

It’s been known since he entered the league that Luka Dončić’s time was coming. He’s been talked about as a future face of the NBA and prematurely anointed as an MVP favorite; it’s understood these things will happen, if not yet, then at some point soon.

It justified the pressure heaped upon him. It’s why the conversations about his conditioning or ball dominance, even when they were simplistic and crude, felt more like rites of passage than punching down. Dončić’s eminence could be described in future tense because there was such certainty it would become present.

Well, Luka Dončić is here now. And he’ll be here for a long time more.

Luka Dončić is now. He’s here. And though he faces a formidable challenge in the conference finals, it’s hard to bet against him. Because the future isn’t just inevitable; it has emerged in earnest. Don’t doubt this dude, someone who was ready to besiege and overwrite what the Suns threw at him up until the moment, far too early, that Phoenix made it clear it offered no challenge.

The Warriors will provide that in this coming series. They deserve the respect that Phoenix ultimately didn’t. Still, would you bet against Dončić? It’s a fraught proposition, one that comes at your own peril, betting against someone who has solidified his stature in this league. He’s not just some future king. He’s presently, at this moment, one of the faces running it.
